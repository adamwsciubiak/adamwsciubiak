About Me

I am a psychologist with a strong focus on UX design, cognitive science, and artificial intelligence. I hold a Master's degree in Psychology from the University of Wrocław and a postgraduate degree in UX & Product Design from SWPS University. My professional experience spans psychological diagnostics, emotion regulation, and neuropsychological therapy. Currently, my research focuses on using AI-driven cognitive-behavioral interventions to enhance emotion regulation in adult learners. I am passionate about the intersection of AI and mental health, and I aim to pursue a PhD in this field.

Projects

My ongoing research involves the development of an Affective Intelligent Agent Model for enhancing emotion regulation in adult learners. This AI-driven model adapts to users' emotional states to provide personalized cognitive-behavioral interventions.

For my Master's thesis, I explored the relationship between mindfulness and creativity in yoga practitioners. The research utilized mixed methods, combining Linguistic Inquiry and Word Count (LIWC) analysis with competent judges' ratings to assess verbal creativity.

I worked on a project to measure cognitive effort using EEG and heart rate data from wearable devices. This project was recognized at Brainhack Krakow for its innovative approach to neurotechnology.
